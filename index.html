The Arcade Arrives At Home

Since the early 80's there was a push to bring the arcade experience into the home. In 1989 when the Sega MegaDrive/Genesis was released, the bar was raised to bringing much better renditions however it wasn't quite there yet.

On May 11, 1995 The Sega Saturn brought the true experience to living rooms the world over. A powerful 32-Bit machine created from the backbone of their arcade hardware.

With a cartridge slot on the back adding in extra ram, studios like Capcom, and SNK brought their games home with much more faithful animations and loading times over the competition.

Three Great Games

Thunder Force V

Horizontal Shooter

Thunder Force V is a 1997 Japanese side-scrolling shooter video game developed by Technosoft for the Sega Saturn. It is the fifth game in the Thunder Force series. Unlike previous games in the series, Thunder Force V uses polygons to model the larger enemy ships and some of the scenery, in addition to sprites.

Panzer Dragoon II Zwei

Rail Shooter

Panzer Dragoon II Zwei is a 1996 rail shooter game for the Sega Saturn, published by Sega and developed by Sega's Team Andromeda studio. It is the sequel to Panzer Dragoon; it follows Jean-Luc Lundi, who rides a dragon he names Lagi.

Sega Rally Championship

Rally Racing

Sega Rally Championship is a 1994 racing video game developed by Sega AM3 and published by Sega. Originally released for arcades using the Sega Model 2 board, it was converted to the Sega Saturn in 1995
